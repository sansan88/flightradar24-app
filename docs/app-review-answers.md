# Apple App Review — Answers (Paid Content / Services)

*Date: July 19, 2026 — App: SkyPi (`ch.sansan.flightradar`)*

Answers provided to the Apple App Review team regarding paid content,
services, and accounts.

---

**1) Does your app access any paid content or services?**

No. SkyPi does not access any paid content or services. The app is completely
free, contains no in-app purchases, no subscriptions, no ads, and no
monetization of any kind. It displays flight data received by the user's own
ADS-B receiver (e.g., a Raspberry Pi running dump1090/readsb) on their local
home network. The only external services the app contacts are free, publicly
available ones: adsbdb.com (free public API for aircraft/route lookups, no
API key or registration required), Swisstopo/OpenStreetMap map tiles, and the
public MeteoSwiss precipitation radar — none of which involve any payment.

**2) What are the paid content or services, and what are the costs?**

Not applicable — there is no paid content and there are no paid services.
There is nothing to buy inside or outside the app. (The ADS-B receiver
hardware is the user's own personal equipment, set up independently of the
app; the app itself neither sells nor requires it and has no commercial
relationship with any hardware vendor.)

**3) Do individual customers pay for the content or services?**

No. Individual users pay nothing. The app and all content shown in it are
free of charge.

**4) If no, does a company or organization pay for the content or services?**

No. No company or organization pays for any content or services either.
SkyPi is a private, non-commercial open-source hobby project
(source code: <https://github.com/sansan88/flightradar24-app>). All data
sources used are free public services.

**5) Where do they pay, and what's the payment method?**

Not applicable — no payments exist anywhere. There is no payment flow inside
the app, no external purchase or subscription website, and no payment method
of any kind.

**6) If users create an account to use your app, are there fees involved?**

Users do not create an account at all. The app has no user accounts, no
registration, no login, and no authentication of any kind — it works entirely
without sign-up, and therefore no fees are involved. (This is also stated in
our privacy policy, see [PRIVACY.md](../PRIVACY.md).) The only configuration
is entering the local IP address of the user's own receiver, which is stored
locally on the device.
